Instructions and policy of publication in the Journal of the Faculty of the Future Humanitarian University:

1.Commitment to scientific methodology in writing research and following the prevailing methodological principles and norms.

2.The research must not be published or received from a scientific effort prior to the research, taking into account the intellectual property rights and copyrights contained in the Iraqi Author Protection Law No. (3) of 1971.

3.The first page of the research should include the full title of the research, the name of the research, the degree, the place of work, and the date of completion of the research, and a brief scientific biography of the research shall be attached to the research.

4.Tables, appendices, and index references are placed at the end of the research.

5.Scientific research is approved in Arabic and English.

6.The research is sent to the journal through the website.

7.The size of the page on which the research is printed should be (A4)

8.Leave a space of 2 hm to keep the page away from all four sides.

9.The research is printed in Arial font, size 16 on the Word system, and the spacing between the lines is (one and a half lines) and the margin font size is 13.

10.The number of research pages should not exceed 25, including references and appendices.

11.Tables and illustrative materials should be well prepared and printed on appropriate polished or transparent paper and an appropriate size for the printing area on the journal page with a maximum of (12 * 16) cm per page, with the presentation of the originals of the images and figures.

12.Tables and figures are numbered respectively according to their appearance in the research, and they are provided with titles, and each of them is indicated in the same sequence and submitted by separate sheets.

13.Always use only SI units (Standard International Units, Si Units)

14.Abbreviations are not used in the title of the research, or in the abstract, except for abbreviations for units of measurement.

15.Abbreviations are used in the titles of scientifically agreed periodicals (Lewis & Co, Periodicals, Scientific of list Word the London).

16.Internationally standardized abbreviations are used both for units of measurement and for other common abbreviations.

17.Unpublished references are not accepted, and references accepted for publication, specified for the volume number in which they will appear, can be accepted.

18.In writing references, both in text and in the bibliography, the Harvard method is used, in which the author's name is linked to the year of publication according to CBE, Style Manual Committee, 1983.

19.The source is indicated according to its appearance in the body of the research with serial numbers placed in parentheses.

20.Arrange references in the list in alphabetical order by authors' names and years of publication.

21.Margins are placed at the end of the research and in sequential numbers.

22.All published research and studies are subject to scientific evaluation inside or outside Iraq from specialists with research experience and distinguished scientific standing.

23.The journal uses at least two arbitrators for each research, and the editor-in-chief may choose a third arbitrator in the event that the research is rejected by one of the arbitrators, and the researcher apologizes for not publishing the research in the event of rejection by the arbitrators.

24.The editorial board has the right to the preliminary examination of the research and determine its eligibility for arbitration, and the opinion of the arbitrators is mandatory for the editor-in-chief.

25.The editor-in-chief may advise the author of the research that is not acceptable for publication in the opinion of the arbitrators or his abstract upon his request without mentioning the names of the arbitrators and without any obligation to respond to the defenses of the author of the research.

26.Priority in publishing accepted research for publication is given to researchers affiliated with the Faculty, especially those related to sustainable development studies.

27.The research may not be published in another international journal after its publication in the journal is approved.

28.From the scientific journal to re-publish the research on paper or electronically that it previously published, without the need for the permission of the researcher, and it has the right to allow others to include its research in various databases, whether for a fee or without charge.

29.The editor-in-chief is keen to inform the author of the research of the validity of the research for publication within two weeks of receiving the responses of the arbitrators.

30.If the journal decides that the researcher publishes the research in paper or electronically before, at, or after submitting it to the journal, the journal has the right to deprive him of future publication in a journal for a period of not less than one year, according to what the editorial board of the journal deems and notifies the party in which it was published.

31.Do not return research submitted to its author, whether published or not.

32.The journal adopts the principle of self-financing and determines the publication fees considering the prevailing prices.

33.The journal checks the plagiarism rates of research submitted for publication in the specialized electronic program (Plagiarism Checker) - (Turnitin), to ensure that the research does not publish partially or completely plagiarized text.

Ethical Guidelines for Publishing :

The guidelines contained in this document have been revised by the Future Journal of Law.

Introduction

Future Journal for Humanities serves society as a whole in several ways, including publishing research and the editorial board of the journal is responsible for setting and maintaining the guiding principles for the selection and acceptance of papers submitted to that journal. The editorial board derives these principles generally from the journal's definition of its scope and the editorial board's perception of the standards of quality and sustainability of scientific work and its presentation.

This journal stems from a desire to identify perceived benefits to society and the profession as a whole and to limit actions that may serve the narrow self-interest of individuals. The advancement of science requires the exchange of knowledge between individuals, although doing so may sometimes entail giving up some immediate personal advantages.

With these ideas in mind, the editorial board of the journal provides a set of ethical guidelines for people involved in the publication of papers, specifically for editorial staff and authors. These guidelines are presented not in the sense that there is any immediate crisis in ethical conduct, but from the conviction that observing high ethical standards is vital for the entire journal and should be brought to the attention of all concerned to these standards.

We believe that most of the guidelines now presented are already understood and shared by most experienced researchers. However, it may be of great benefit to those relatively new to research.
